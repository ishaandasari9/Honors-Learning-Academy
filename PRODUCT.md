# Honors Learning Academy — PRODUCT.md

**Register:** brand (the website *is* the product — marketing, trust, and conversion drive every decision)

## What it is
Honors Learning Academy (HLA) is a student-founded nonprofit in Waxhaw / Marvin, North Carolina that provides **free** charitable tutoring to K–12 students. Tutoring is peer-led: high-achieving high-school and college students tutor younger students, both online and in person. HLA is pursuing 501(c)(3) status.

Beyond core school subjects (math, science, English, social studies), HLA offers **specialized tracks** most peer tutoring never touches: computer science, business, pre-med, and pre-law (e.g. public speaking, legal literacy). This breadth is the organization's real differentiator.

## Two audiences, served distinctly
1. **Families & students seeking help** — parents deciding whether to trust HLA with their child, and students who want a tutor. They need: *Is it really free? What grades/subjects? Online or near me? Is it safe and legitimate?* The site must answer these in the first screen.
2. **Tutors / members** — high-achieving students who tutor and earn verified volunteer hours (once 501(c)(3) is granted). They need: a clear path to apply, what they gain, and a portal to log hours.

These two journeys get their own dedicated pages so neither is diluted.

## Jobs the site must do
- Let families **request a tutor** (native form).
- Let students **apply to become a tutor** (native form).
- Let members **log volunteer hours** through a guided, conversational logger and see their running total.
- Give an admin a **manual editor** to update content (stats, copy, links, contact, socials, section visibility) without touching code.
- Establish **trust**: named (first-name) leadership, real contact, honest stats, clear free/where/grades messaging, a privacy note for a nonprofit serving minors.

## Brand & tone
Prestigious but warm. Institutional credibility a cautious parent trusts, with enough energy that an ambitious teenager wants to be part of it. Editorial, calm, confident. Never hype-y, never "AI-startup." Think a respected university's brand system, not a SaaS landing page.

Voice: plain, concrete, specific. "Free, always" not "unlock your potential." Names real subjects and real outcomes. No buzzwords, no rule-of-three padding, no em dashes.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)
- The previous HLA site: dark navy + glowing gold/blue "futuristic academic," interactive 3D crest, heavy scroll-hijacking. The founder calls it "AI slop." Do not return to it.
- Generic nonprofit reflex: stock smiling-students photos, teal + white, hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids.
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, neon-on-dark, side-stripe accent borders.

## Benchmark
actionforgood.us — clean, warm, editorial, trustworthy. HLA should beat it: more distinctive typography, stronger hierarchy, a clearer two-audience split, and real interactive features (forms, hours portal, admin) that Action for Good doesn't have.

## Strategic principles
1. **Trust before polish.** Every screen should give a parent a reason to believe HLA is real, safe, and free.
2. **Two doors, clearly labeled.** "Get a tutor" and "Become a tutor" are the two primary actions; never make a visitor guess which is theirs.
3. **Honest numbers.** Stats are editable and should reflect reality, not invented hype. When a number is small, use language, not figures.
4. **Free is the headline.** "100% free" appears above the fold, not buried.
5. **Specialized tracks are the hook.** Pre-law, pre-med, business, CS set HLA apart. Feature them.
